**Data source:** DataForSEO (Google Ads + Labs API) | **Market:** US | **Date:** Feb 2026

## TL;DR — Best Bang for Buck

The highest-volume Gibson Les Paul keywords (9,900–22,200/mo) are **fully saturated** by Wildwood, CME, Ishibashi, Gibson.com, Sweetwater, and Reverb. You won't outrank them on product pages.

**Your edge:** Nobody is creating collector-focused educational content. The keywords below are high-intent, low-competition, and directly attract Marcus and Derek — people who are *researching* before spending $4K–$20K.

## What to AVOID (Competitor-Saturated)

These keywords are dominated by Wildwood, CME, Ishibashi, Sweetwater, Reverb, and Gibson.com with massive domain authority. Don't build content strategy around ranking for these — let them come naturally through product pages.

| Keyword | Vol/mo | Why Skip |
|---------|--------|----------|
| gibson les paul custom | 9,900 | Gibson.com, Sweetwater, GC own this. |
| les paul standard | 22,200+ | Same — brand term dominated by Gibson. |
| gibson les paul studio | 6,600 | Gibson + big retailers. |
| used gibson les paul | 2,400 | Reverb, eBay, GC dominate marketplace intent. |
| gibson les paul for sale | 1,600 | Marketplace keywords — Reverb eats this. |

**Exception:** Target these on your actual product/listing pages with unique content (weight, tone description, provenance, honest flaws). Don't build standalone blog posts for them.
